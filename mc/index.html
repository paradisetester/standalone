Managed care is the dominant delivery system for the Medicaid program today. Thirty eight states, the District of Columbia and Puerto Rico contract with risk-based managed care organizations (MCOs) to deliver the majority of benefits to their Medicaid beneficiaries. Comprehensive MCOs typically provide all acute, primary and specialty medical services; some also cover behavioral health (BH) and long-term services and supports (LTSS).

This forecast is designed to help understand the timing of states' current Medicaid managed care contracts and anticipated procurement. The forecast will be updated periodically.

The data included in the forecast were obtained through a review of active comprehensive Medicaid managed care contracts and additional state research. A number of these comprehensive managed care programs integrate LTSS and BH benefits. We have reflected where LTSS and BH benefits are provided through the comprehensive, risk-based managed care program where known, but this forecast does not attempt to capture separate managed care contracts for LTSS and BH.
